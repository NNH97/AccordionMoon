The moon is a small planet orbiting around Earth and always remains a romantic highlight in the night sky. When the moon is full, it becomes a beautiful natural light, shining over the earth.

The moon has fascinated humans for thousands of years. It is celebrated as a symbol of purity, great dreams, and creativity. Perhaps, that's why the moon always inspires writers, poets, and researchers.

The moon has a significant influence on humans. It is considered a crucial factor in regulating symptoms of mental illness, such as anxiety, depression, and sleep disorders.

The moon has strange and elusive characteristics. It has no atmosphere, no water, and no life. However, it has a surface covered by various hills, valleys, grasslands, and even icy areas.

The moon has a rotation around Earth, known as the "lunar day and night." This period lasts approximately 29.5 days and causes fascinating phenomena, such as lunar eclipses, lunar transits, and lunar occultations.
